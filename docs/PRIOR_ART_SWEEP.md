# Prior-art sweep — adversarial online literature check (2026-07-22)

Purpose: before submission, attack every headline claim of MANUSCRIPT v6 with the
published literature, the way a hostile referee would. Each claim gets a verdict:

- **SAFE** — no prior work does this; claim can stand as stated.
- **MUST-CITE** — prior work overlaps or is the nearest neighbor; cite and position.
- **CLAIM-ADJUST** — the claim as previously worded overstated novelty; reworded in v6.

Method: targeted web searches + full-text reads (PDFs pulled and text-extracted)
of the six closest papers, plus two new experiments run in response
(single-vs-aggregate split; f12 forced-mixing test).

## The two big collisions, and what they change

### Gerlach & Altmann 2013 (PRX 3, 021006)
Closest prior work in spirit: a two-class stochastic model — "(i) a finite number
of core-words which have higher frequency and do not affect the probability of a
new word to be used; and (ii) the remaining virtually infinite number of
noncore-words" — that generalizes Zipf AND Heaps to two scaling regimes and fits
databases from 10³ to 10¹¹ words with language-dependent, database-independent
parameters.

What they do not have: any transition-width object (their crossover is a sharp
parameter), any residual-space diagnostic, any width scaling law, any
histogram→curve prediction, any labeled test of what the boundary is. Their core
is a **fixed-size set per language**; our measured seam location and width both
**scale with V** (s = 0.012·V, R² 0.967). A fixed core predicts a fixed crossover
rank across corpora of one language — the 25-corpus English panel refutes that
(s spans 44→304 across V 3.8k→29k). We cite them as the strongest prior
two-population model and present the s-law as the measurement their model family
must now reproduce.

### Williams, Bagrow, Danforth & Dodds 2015 (PRE 91, 052811)
The adversarial hypothesis: two-regime structure is an artifact of *aggregating*
texts ("text mixing"), with the break at b ≈ N_avg (mean per-text vocabulary),
and the core-lexicon reading "in strong need of reevaluation." They explicitly
extend this to single texts ("an individual text such as this may in of itself
present a scaling break that has resulted from [internal] text mixing").

Two new tests, both run for this sweep:

1. **Single-vs-aggregate split** (existing f2 panel, 15 single continuous works
   vs 10 collections): mean s/V 0.0119 vs 0.0113, Welch p = 0.19, Mann-Whitney
   p = 0.17, bootstrap CI on the difference [−0.0015, +0.0002]. The groups
   interleave completely. If mixing drove the seam, the Bible (66 books, many
   authors) and Shakespeare (37 plays) should differ systematically from
   Wealth of Nations — they don't. Direction of the (non-significant) trend is
   *opposite* to mixing-inflation.
2. **f12 forced-mixing experiment** (`experiments/f12_forced_mixing/`): 14
   single works fit individually, then in forced concatenations of m = 2, 4, 7,
   14 with one identical fitter. Williams predicts the break migrates toward
   N_avg (~10⁴) and grows with m; the s-law predicts s/V ≈ 0.012 at every m
   with k ≪ N_avg. **Result (2026-07-22): the s-law wins on both axes.** Median
   s/V by m: 0.0119 / 0.0127 / 0.0128 / 0.0123 / 0.0120 — flat across a 14×
   change in aggregation. Median k/N_avg: 0.021 → 0.079 — the seam never
   approaches the predicted mixing break; its mild growth tracks V, not N_avg.
   Pooled s ~ V regression over all 27 fits: β = 0.977, R² = 0.983 — the
   tightest width-law measurement in the project, with forced mixtures landing
   on the same line as single novels.

Also note the objects differ: Williams' break lives at rank ≈ N_avg (thousands
to tens of thousands, corpus-relative); our seam sits at k ~ 10²–10³ with a
width, inside what their analysis treats as the unbroken Zipf/Simon head. Citing
them and reporting both tests turns the strongest published attack on
two-population readings into supporting evidence for the seam's intrinsic-ness.

## Other positioning notes

- **Montemurro 2001** (Physica A 300, 567): showed ZM "can only describe the
  statistical behavior of a restricted fraction of words" and proposed a
  Tsallis-form generalization with two regimes. Cite in §3.1 (the restricted-
  range observation is the 2001 ancestor of our residual) and §3.7 (λ-ZM vs
  Tsallis family — different closed forms; ours preserves the ZM head exactly).
- **Piantadosi 2014**: "there is considerable structure to the word frequency
  distribution beyond the fit of the Zipf-Mandelbrot equation." §3.1 now opens
  from this documented fact; our step is the symbolic diagnosis of that
  structure and the demonstration that the diagnosis is scoring-conditioned.
- **Font-Clos, Boleda & Corral 2013** (NJP 15, 093033): scale-invariant shape
  under text growth; exponent drift with L is "an artifact of the increasing
  weight of a second regime"; β = 1/α cited there as derived in earlier work.
  Cite in §3.4 (c trajectories = our parametric window on the same growth
  process) and §3.6 (Heaps duality is confirmation, not discovery). Their
  scale-invariance also predicts fixed-rank objects at fixed relative frequency
  — which makes the V-scaling of s a *discriminating* measurement, not a
  corollary.
- **Bernhardsson, da Rocha & Minnhagen 2009** (NJP 11, 123015): size-dependent
  word-frequency distribution (the "metabook" picture) with γ(L), α(L). The
  b(T), c(T) trajectories of f6 are the ZM-parameter version of this
  observation; cite in §3.4.
- **Mačutek 2022** (JQL 29, 405): ZM parameter "explosion" b,c→∞ with b/c
  converging = convergence to a geometric distribution (graphemes, species
  data). Opposite corner of parameter space from our corpora (our c stays
  finite and *decreases* with depth), but it is the published proof that ZM
  parameters are estimation-fragile — supports treating (b,c) as diagnostic
  quantities rather than universal constants. Cite in §3.4.
- **Baayen 2001 / Evert & Baroni 2007+**: LNRE (fZM, GIGP) extrapolation of V(N)
  and frequency spectra; binomial interpolation ≡ our thinning check. §3.5
  extrapolation results now framed as extending this program to the rank law's
  parameters; References gains both.
- **Dębowski 2026** (arXiv 2512.13491): deductive chain Zipf → Heaps → Hilberg
  → neural scaling. Cite in §4.4 as the theory-side counterpart of the L3
  learning-frontier observation.
- **Berman 2025** (arXiv 2511.21060, two-stage symbolic processes): geometric
  mechanism for Zipf-like laws without linguistics; tangential — not cited.
- **Ferrer-i-Cancho & Solé 2001**: already cited (kernel-lexicon hypothesis,
  exponents ≈ −1/−2). Kept; §4.3 sharpens the relation: their kernel is the
  hypothesis, our gate/k/s program is the measurement.

## Addendum (2026-07-25): the f14 reversal

The sweep table above inferred that Gerlach-Altmann's fixed core "predicts a
crossover at fixed rank," making the s-law a discriminator against their model
class. **Simulation refuted the inference** (`experiments/f14_ga_break/`):
their model, with their published English constants, obeys the width law
(s/V ≈ 0.0122 across V = 16k–88k), as does a coreless one-class
decaying-innovation variant (0.0118 — the real-language value). A genuinely
single-regime constant-innovation process fits a distinct stable constant
(0.0101), and surnames another (0.0266): the width constant is a
family-specific fingerprint, not a fitting artifact, and natural language
sits on the decaying-innovation family's value. Manuscript §3.3/§4.1/§4.3
corrected accordingly: the width law's status as a *law* strengthens (a shared
invariant every adequate account inherits); its use as a mechanism
discriminator is retired. What discriminates accounts is the boundary's
identity (§3.6) and depth behaviour (§3.4).
