# Audit report: Zipf-Mandelbrot misspecification package (manuscript v5.1)

## High-level audit summary
The package supports a publishable paper, but not the paper currently written. The strongest scoped version is an empirical paper about a reproducible, chart-dependent residual diagnostic for single-ZM misspecification, its localization to a two-regime seam, and the way a smooth two-regime family absorbs that residual. That core is real and well backed by the bundled CSVs. The main direction-changing findings from this audit are that several headline v5.1 claims lean on evidence that is blocked, missing from the package, or theoretically overstated: the decoupled-erf gate-family sweep that now anchors the abstract and title is not present in bundled canonical outputs; the information-geometric / manifold language repeatedly overstates what the experiments establish; Table 3 is semantically mislabeled; Table 5 has the decomposition-improvement sign reversed; hierarchical pooling is described incorrectly; and a few robustness / transfer statements overclaim beyond saved data. My recommendation is to scope Paper 1 around the residual diagnostic, seam localization, absorption, and carefully worded smooth-family evidence, while moving PMF work to Paper 2 and cutting or rerunning the decoupled-gate and blocked-parameter claims.

## Scope recommendations
Paper 1 should be scoped around a narrower, stronger claim set. The defensible central contribution is: deterministic symbolic search on single-ZM residuals reveals a reproducible Bregman-type diagnostic pattern in a specific normalized residual coordinate; non-linguistic and synthetic controls localize that pattern to fitting one curve across a two-regime seam; and a smooth two-regime family absorbs the residual in a way MOEZipf does not. That is already enough for a good paper.

I would put the following in Paper 1: Sections 3.1 to 3.4, the mechanism-absorption core of 3.7, the analytic seam expansion in 3.8 but with toned-down geometric rhetoric, the high-c and low-c recovery evidence from 3.9 only if explicitly labeled historical-smooth-family rather than decoupled-erf-canonical, the multilingual 7/7 / 29/29 extension after fixing Table 3 and removing the theorem-heavy recentering claim from the main line, and the widened-grammar 10b diagnostic because it strongly supports the metric-sensitive low-c span story.

I would move the entire PMF arc, by default, to Paper 2: Sections 2.16, 3.11, and the supporting experiments 7a through 9. The PMF work is real and interesting, but it is large enough to be its own paper, and it introduces a second objective family, additional regularization choices, and a distinct likelihood-mechanism frontier that distracts from the cleaner Paper 1 contribution.

I would cut from Paper 1 unless rerun: the decoupled gate-family sweep as a headline result, the logistic synthetic gate recovery, the blocked 5c parameter-control table, any transferred-sign evidence from experiment 4, the unsupported WLS winner-stability claim, and the unsupported 10a Bible-transfer claim.

A better section order for the scoped Paper 1 is: (1) residual diagnostic and c-organization, (2) robustness and non-linguistic / function-word / synthetic controls, (3) smooth-family absorption vs MOE, (4) analytic seam expansion, (5) simulation recovery and low-c span structure, (6) multilingual and grammar-widening extension, (7) limitations and what is and is not intrinsic.

A better title would avoid “information geometry” and “canonical erf mechanism”. Two reasonable options are:
1. “A symbolic residual diagnostic for Zipf-Mandelbrot misspecification reveals a two-regime lexical seam”
2. “Bregman-type residual structure in single Zipf-Mandelbrot fits localizes a two-regime seam in word-frequency data”

The abstract should frame the paper as an empirical residual-diagnostic and mechanism-localization study, not as a settled information-geometric theory of the lexicon.

## Priority ranking
Essential before submission: remove the false convex-conjugation / manifold-geometry framing; fix Table 3 and Table 5; correct the same-exponent control, hierarchical-pooling statement, manual POS discrepancy, and unsupported transfer claims; decide whether decoupled gate-family evidence will actually be bundled. If not, strip it from the title, abstract, and central framing.

Nice to have for the same paper cycle: rerun WLS robustness, produce a cleaner statement of the x^x-√x recentering result as a theorem or supplement, and regenerate 5a under the true decoupled-erf canon so the simulation-recovery story aligns with the claimed canonical model.

Future work / Paper 2: the PMF family, hierarchy / asymmetry / hybrid frontier, and cleaner likelihood validation under train/validation/test or repeated resampling.

## Bottom line
The package contains a strong paper, but the strongest bundled paper is narrower than v5.1. The publishable core is the residual-diagnostic / seam-localization / mechanism-absorption story. The current title, abstract, and several late-added headline claims should be revised to match the evidence actually bundled for audit.
